For most capitals and cities in western countries, museums of history, arts, and nature are basic configurations in which these cities offer. Ottawa would be a good example of such, being the capital of Canada and having over 10 museums. Out of all of the museums in Ottawa, visitors always seem to be going back to the same one, The Canadian Museum of Nature. This particular museum has had an 8 year, multi-million dollar renovation. To add, the Museum of Nature is also the largest museum of nature in Canada. It's located in an old Victorian building in downtown Ottawa.

This year, students from the Media Production and Design Program in Carleton University is fortunate to collaborate with the museum to design interactive activities for the different exhibit hosted in the museum. One of the exhibit held during the year is the "Butterflies in Flight," the prototype that we created was based on this exhibit. The prototype selected will be a VR game that will be placed in the first room of the butterfly exhibit. The VR prototype is designed to enhance the butterfly exhibit by giving guests a different perspective and it actually puts the user into world of a butterfly.
